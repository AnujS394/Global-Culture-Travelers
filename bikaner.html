🐫 Bikaner – The Desert Jewel of Camels, Culture & Crunch

Nestled in the heart of the Thar Desert, Bikaner is where royal heritage, fiery snacks, camel parades, and fortified architecture thrive. Known as the “Camel Country of India”, it blends desert elegance with rich Marwari culture.

🎉 Major Cultural Festivals & Events

🐪 Bikaner Camel Festival

When: January

Where: Junagarh Fort, Camel Farm

Celebrating the pride of the desert – camels – with races, dances, beauty contests, and fire shows. Camels are decorated with vibrant embroidery, beads, and bridles.

🎨 Karni Mata Fair

When: March–April & Sept–Oct (Navratri)

Where: Deshnok

Held at the famous “Rat Temple”, where thousands of rats are worshipped. Devotees attend rituals, bhajans, and offer prasad.

🪔 Diwali & Dev Deepawali

Where: Junagarh Fort, Kote Gate, Temples

Bikaner lights up with rangolis, lamps, and folk music. Palaces and havelis open for public celebrations.

🎭 Gangaur Festival

When: March–April

Where: Old City

Celebrating love, devotion, and womanhood with processions of idols, folk dress, and community prayers.

🧵 Utsav Handicraft & Food Festival

When: Dec–Jan

Where: Rani Bazar Grounds

Explore local crafts, camel-hide accessories, tie-dye fashion, and spicy snacks. A winter cultural carnival.

🥘 Taste of Bikaner – Spices, Snacks & Sweets

Famous Dishes:

  • Bikaneri Bhujia – Iconic spicy snack
  • Pyaaz Kachori – Spicy onion filling inside crispy pastry
  • Kachori-Sabzi – A street food favorite
  • Gatte ki Sabzi – Besan dumplings in curry
  • Pakodi Kadhi – Tangy yogurt-based delight
  • Bajra Roti with Lehsun Chutney – Desert staple combo
  • Rasgulla (Bikaneri) – Grainy and soft dessert
  • Ghevar & Boondi Laddu – Festive sweets
  • Mawa Mishri & Rabri Faluda – Summer coolers

Popular Food Hotspots:

  • Chhotu Motu Joshi Sweet Shop – Rasgullas, kachoris
  • Bikaner Bhujia Bhandar – Original bhujia outlet
  • Heeralal’s Restaurant – Famous for thalis
  • Gallops Restaurant – Fusion cuisine near the fort
  • Laxmi Mishthan Bhandar – Sweets and snacks
  • Kote Gate & Station Road – Street food heaven

🏯 Experience Bikaner Beyond Festivals

  • Junagarh Fort: Unconquered fort with palaces, art, and weapons gallery
  • Karni Mata Temple: Famous rat temple – a rare spiritual site
  • Lalgarh Palace: Indo-European royal residence turned heritage hotel
  • National Camel Research Centre: Learn about camel breeds and try camel milk kulfi
  • Lakshmi Nath Temple: Ancient Vishnu-Lakshmi shrine
  • Ganga Singh Museum: Desert life and Bikaner royal history
  • Kote Gate, MG Road & Rani Bazaar: Great for camel leather, mojris, snacks, and souvenirs
