State-wise Cultural Don’ts in India

Each Indian state has unique customs. Avoid these cultural missteps while exploring:

🇮🇳 General Don’ts Across India

  • Don’t point your feet at people or religious objects.
  • Don’t touch books or people with your feet—it’s considered disrespectful.
  • Don’t display affection (hug/kiss) in public, especially in rural areas.
  • Don’t photograph people without asking permission, especially in sensitive areas.
  • Don’t enter homes, temples, or mosques with shoes on.
  • Don’t criticize religion or caste—it's a sensitive subject in many communities.
  • Don’t hand things to others with your left hand—use your right hand or both hands.

🇮🇳 Madhya Pradesh

  • Don’t enter tribal villages without permission or guidance.
  • Don’t touch sacred tribal artifacts or totems without asking.
  • Avoid loud conversations or phone calls in religious or forest areas.

🇮🇳 Odisha

  • Don’t wear revealing clothes at Jagannath Temple or Konark Sun Temple.
  • Don’t consume meat or alcohol near holy sites like Puri.
  • Avoid disrespecting traditional Odissi performances or Pattachitra artists.

🇮🇳 Assam

  • Don’t disrespect Bihu festivals or treat them as just entertainment.
  • Avoid using plastic near the Kaziranga and other eco-sensitive zones.
  • Don’t mock or misrepresent tribal customs or headgear.

🇮🇳 West Bengal

  • Don’t question religious practices during Durga Puja.
  • Avoid littering or spitting near public ghats or pandals.
  • Don’t disrupt classical performances or folk theatre with noise or phones.

🇮🇳 Kerala

  • Don’t enter temples like Sabarimala with improper attire.
  • Don’t mock or interrupt Kathakali, Theyyam or Kalaripayattu sessions.
  • Avoid public intimacy, especially in conservative towns or beaches.

🇮🇳 Rajasthan

  • Don’t damage or deface heritage sites like forts and havelis.
  • Don’t joke about Rajasthani dialects or rural traditions.
  • Avoid entering temples without covering your head (in rural areas).
