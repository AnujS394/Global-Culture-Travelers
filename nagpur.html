🍊 Nagpur – The Orange City of Heritage, Harmony & Heat

Nagpur, the winter capital of Maharashtra, is a radiant fusion of Vidarbha's folk traditions, Buddhist heritage, modern civic pride, and spicy Saoji cuisine. Known for its oranges and its central location in India (Zero Mile), Nagpur thrives as a cultural crossroad of north, south, and tribal India.

🎉 Major Cultural Festivals & Events

🕯 Dhamma Chakra Pravartan Din

When: October 14

Where: Deekshabhoomi

Commemorates Dr. B.R. Ambedkar’s conversion to Buddhism with lakhs of devotees in peaceful ceremonies, chanting, and meditation.

🎭 Nagpur Mahotsav

When: January

Where: Kasturchand Park, Yashwant Stadium

A folk-cultural extravaganza with Lavani, Bhajan, theatre, food, and musical nights featuring both regional and national artists.

📚 Orange City Literature Festival

When: December

Where: Suresh Bhat Auditorium & Online

A literary celebration with poets, screenwriters, authors, and readers through discussions, launches, and performances.

🎨 Kalidas Festival

When: November

Where: Ramtek

Celebrates Kalidasa’s classical legacy with Sanskrit plays, Kathak, Bharatanatyam, and music in a scenic historical setting.

🎊 Ganesh Utsav

When: August–September

Where: Mahal, Sitabuldi, Shankar Nagar, Itwari

Eco-friendly idols, themed pandals, community celebrations, and energetic processions mark this major festival.

🌺 Marbat Festival

When: August (Shravan month)

Where: Old Nagpur

A uniquely Nagpur festival where effigies of evil (Marbat) are paraded to ward off negativity — rich in satire and tribal tradition.

🥘 Taste of Nagpur – Bold, Spicy & Flavorful

  • Saoji Chicken/Mutton – Bold, black masala meat curry
  • Tarri Poha – Poha topped with spicy chickpea curry
  • Patodi Rassa – Besan patties in spicy gravy
  • Zunka Bhakri – Dry gram curry with millet roti
  • Shev Bhaji – Thick sev in fiery curry
  • Orange Burfi – Signature sweet made with Nagpuri oranges
  • Til-Gul Ladoo – Sesame-jaggery sweet for Sankranti

🍴 Popular Food Hotspots

  • Haldiram’s (Original outlets)
  • Saoji Bhojanalayas – Gokulpeth, Itwari
  • Variety Square & Sadar for biryani, chaats
  • Panchsheel Square food stalls
  • Sitabuldi Main Road – sandwiches, lassi
  • Lokmat Square cafés and dessert places

🏛 Experience Nagpur Beyond Festivals

  • Deekshabhoomi: Global Buddhist pilgrimage and peace site
  • Zero Mile Stone: Geographic heart of India
  • Futala & Ambazari Lakes: Scenic sunset spots and recreation
  • Ramtek Temple & Kalidas Smarak: Blend of myth, poetry, and hills
  • Cotton Market & Dharampeth: Sarees, tribal art, orange-based goods
  • Nagpur Metro Cultural Stations: Art-rich metro hubs with murals
