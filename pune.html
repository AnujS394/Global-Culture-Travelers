🎓 Pune – Maharashtra’s Cultural Heartbeat

Pune, often called the “Oxford of the East”, is a charming blend of Maratha heritage, spiritual depth, artistic expression, and youthful vibrance. With its legacy of education, classical music, theatre, and innovation, Pune is where tradition gracefully meets modern energy.

🎉 Major Cultural Festivals & Events

🪕 Sawai Gandharva Bhimsen Mahotsav

When: December (Annually)

Where: Maharashtriya Mandal Ground, Pune

One of India’s most revered classical music festivals, featuring overnight performances of ragas, vocalists, and instrumentalists.

🎬 Pune International Film Festival (PIFF)

When: January

Where: NFAI, Bal Gandharva Auditorium, Multiplexes

A major event featuring international cinema, student films, panel discussions, and masterclasses by renowned filmmakers.

📚 Pune Literary Festival

When: December

Where: YASHADA, MIT Campuses

Celebrating literature through poetry readings, book launches, writer meetups, and creative workshops.

🕯️ Ganesh Chaturthi – Pune Style

When: August–September

Where: Dagdusheth Halwai Ganpati, Kasba Ganpati, Tulshibaug

The most celebrated festival in Pune, marked by community mandals, devotional music, decorations, and grand visarjan processions.

🎭 Shivaji Jayanti & Palkhi Festival

When: February (Shivaji Jayanti), June (Palkhi)

Where: Shaniwar Wada, Alandi–Dehu Routes

Celebrations of heritage and devotion with processions, bhajans, historical storytelling, and vibrant pilgrim culture.

🎨 NH7 Weekender

When: November

Where: Laxmi Lawns or Pune outskirts

India’s happiest music festival with indie, EDM, stand-up comedy, food stalls, and creative installations.

🎶 Vasantotsav

When: February

Where: Sarasbaug or cultural grounds

A tribute to classical and devotional music featuring bhajans, ghazals, and natya sangeet in an elegant setting.

🥘 Taste of Pune – A City of Flavors

  • Misal Pav – Spicy sprout curry with farsan
  • Sabudana Khichdi – A fasting staple with peanuts
  • Poha with Tarri – Light breakfast with spicy curry
  • Bakarwadi – Spiced Maharashtrian snack
  • Mastani – Thick mango milkshake with ice cream
  • Puran Poli – Sweet lentil-stuffed bread
  • Modak – Coconut-jaggery sweet for Ganeshotsav
  • Bhakri-Zunka – Rural dish of millet roti and gram sabzi

🍴 Food Hotspots

🏛 Experience Pune Beyond Festivals

  • Shaniwar Wada: Peshwa-era fort and storytelling
  • Aga Khan Palace: Gandhi’s legacy museum
  • Kelkar Museum: Folk instruments and artifacts
  • Cycle Tours: Sunrise heritage routes
  • Osho Ashram: Spiritual retreats and gardens
  • Live Theatres: Balgandharva & Sudarshan Rangmanch
  • Parvati Hill & Sarasbaug: Temples and city views
