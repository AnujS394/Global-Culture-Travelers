State-wise Cultural Do’s in India

Embrace the richness of each state by respecting and following these cultural practices:

Why Cultural Do’s Matter

When visiting a new place, it's important to show respect and understanding for the local traditions, beliefs, and customs. Doing so not only enhances your experience but also promotes cross-cultural harmony and appreciation.

Essential Cultural Do’s

  • Learn a few basic phrases in the local language, like "Hello", "Thank you", and "Excuse me".
  • Dress modestly and appropriately, especially near religious or sacred sites.
  • Always remove your shoes before entering someone’s home or a temple/mosque.
  • Use your right hand when offering or receiving something.
  • Participate respectfully in local festivals and celebrations.
  • Respect the personal space and customs of locals.
  • Be punctual if you're attending traditional or formal events.
  • Ask before taking photos, especially of individuals or religious ceremonies.

Tips for Responsible Tourism

  • Support local businesses, artists, and guides.
  • Avoid touching or climbing on heritage structures and sculptures.
  • Follow local environmental practices – reduce plastic usage and waste.
  • Be patient and open-minded when encountering unfamiliar traditions or beliefs.

🇮🇳 Madhya Pradesh

  • Greet locals respectfully, especially elders in villages.
  • Take permission before entering tribal areas or photographing people.
  • Explore local handicrafts and support tribal markets.

🇮🇳 Odisha

  • Remove shoes before entering any temple premises.
  • Attend cultural festivals like Rath Yatra and support traditional art.
  • Try authentic Odia food with respect for vegetarian customs in religious areas.

🇮🇳 Assam

  • Say “Namaskar” and learn basic Assamese greetings.
  • Respect nature and local communities in eco-tourism zones.
  • Appreciate Bihu dances and support handloom products.

🇮🇳 West Bengal

  • Attend Durga Puja with respectful attire and behavior.
  • Learn about Tagore and Bengali cultural icons.
  • Enjoy local sweets like rasgulla and sandesh politely if offered.

🇮🇳 Kerala

  • Wear traditional attire when entering temples.
  • Take backwater tours and support eco-friendly tourism.
  • Respect Ayurveda and holistic health customs.

🇮🇳 Rajasthan

  • Dress modestly when visiting forts or rural areas.
  • Bargain politely in local bazaars.
  • Attend folk performances and appreciate local music and dance.
