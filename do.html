State-wise Cultural Do’s in India

Embrace the richness of each state by respecting and following these cultural practices:

Why Cultural Do’s Matter

When visiting a new place, it's important to show respect and understanding for the local traditions, beliefs, and customs. Doing so not only enhances your experience but also promotes cross-cultural harmony and appreciation.

Essential Cultural Do’s

  • Learn a few basic phrases in the local language, like "Hello", "Thank you", and "Excuse me".
  • Dress modestly and appropriately, especially near religious or sacred sites.
  • Always remove your shoes before entering someone’s home or a temple/mosque (where required).
  • Use your right hand when offering or receiving something, particularly in South Asian cultures.
  • Participate respectfully in local festivals and celebrations.
  • Respect the personal space and customs of locals.
  • Be punctual if you're attending traditional or formal events.
  • Ask before taking photos, especially of individuals or religious ceremonies.

Tips for Responsible Tourism

  • Support local businesses, artists, and guides.
  • Avoid touching or climbing on heritage structures and sculptures.
  • Learn and follow local environmental practices – reduce plastic usage and waste.
  • Be patient and open-minded when encountering unfamiliar traditions or beliefs.

🇮🇳 Madhya Pradesh

  • Do greet locals respectfully, especially elders in villages.
  • Do take permission before entering tribal areas or photographing people.
  • Do explore local handicrafts and support tribal markets.

🇮🇳 Odisha

  • Do remove shoes before entering any temple premises.
  • Do attend cultural festivals like Rath Yatra and support traditional art.
  • Do try authentic Odia food with respect for vegetarian customs in religious areas.

🇮🇳 Assam

  • Do say “Namaskar” and learn basic Assamese greetings.
  • Do respect nature and local communities in eco-tourism zones.
  • Do appreciate Bihu dances and support handloom products.

🇮🇳 West Bengal

  • Do attend Durga Puja with respectful attire and behavior.
  • Do learn about Tagore and Bengali cultural icons during your visit.
  • Do enjoy local sweets like rasgulla and sandesh politely if offered.

🇮🇳 Kerala

  • Do wear traditional attire when entering temples (mundu for men, saree/salwar for women).
  • Do take backwater tours and support eco-friendly tourism.
  • Do respect Ayurveda and holistic health customs.

🇮🇳 Rajasthan

  • Do dress modestly when visiting forts or rural areas.
  • Do bargain politely in local bazaars—it’s part of the culture.
  • Do attend folk performances and appreciate Rajasthani music and dance.
