🏞 Amravati – Vidarbha’s Cultural Canvas

Amravati, a vibrant district in the Vidarbha region of Maharashtra, blends tribal heritage, freedom movement legacy, religious devotion, and natural charm. Known as the land of goddess Ambadevi, Amravati is where folklore, education, and local flavors come together in harmony.

🎉 Major Cultural Festivals & Events

🕯 Ambadevi Yatra & Navratri Mahotsav

When: September–October (Sharadiya Navratri)

Where: Ambadevi Temple, Gandhi Chowk

Devotional celebration for Goddess Ambadevi with bhajans, folk dances, spiritual discourses, and a large yatra (fair).

🎭 Vidarbha Sanskrutik Mahotsav

When: January / February

Where: University Grounds, Auditoriums

Showcasing tribal arts, Lavani, Bhajans, and Korku dances along with rural crafts and music performances.

🕊 Kranti Din & Gadge Baba Jayanti

When: August 9 & February

Where: Freedom Park, Gadge Baba Memorial

Tribute to Vidarbha's freedom fighters and Sant Gadge Baba, with student rallies, speeches, and cultural gatherings.

📿 Maha Shivratri at Melghat & Chikhaldara Temples

When: February–March

Where: Morshi, Melghat, Chikhaldara

Hilltop temples host rituals, tribal fairs, and eco-spiritual journeys during Maha Shivratri.

🎨 Youth & Tribal Art Festivals

When: November–February

Where: University, Art Colleges, Kala Bhavan

Features Gond and Korku dances, student exhibitions, and folk art celebrating Vidarbha’s culture.

🥘 Taste of Amravati

Famous Dishes:

  • Varhadi Chicken – Spicy red chicken curry
  • Zunka Bhakri – Besan sabzi with jowar roti
  • Shev Bhaji – Sev in onion-tomato gravy
  • Puran Poli – Sweet lentil flatbread
  • Saoji Egg Curry – With traditional Saoji masala
  • Gavhale Kheer – Sweet milk pasta with cardamom
  • Chutneycha Thecha – Fiery green chutney
  • Bhakarwadi & Chakli – Savory dry snacks

Food Hotspots:

  • Laxmi Misal & Snacks
  • Hotel Ramdev – Camp Road
  • Jawahar Road & Lokmat Square Stalls
  • Gopal Sweets & Sainath Bhel
  • Railway Station Food Zone

🌄 Experience Amravati Beyond Festivals

  • Ambadevi & Ekvira Temples: Ancient temples with links to the Ramayana
  • Chikhaldara Hill Station: Only hill station in Vidarbha with coffee plantations and Bhima Kund
  • Melghat Tiger Reserve: Jungle safaris, birdwatching, tribal culture
  • Satidham Temple Complex: Replicas of India’s famous temples
  • Freedom Park & Irwin Square: Historic protest sites now used for cultural shows
  • Kala Bhavan & Art Fairs: Tribal, social, and natural art exhibitions
